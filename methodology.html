Methodology

How The Hechinger Report’s College Financial Health Tracker is built — the data sources, how we filter and normalize them, what’s included, and what’s not.

This tracker combines several federal datasets with original reporting to surface early warning signs of financial distress at four-year, primarily bachelor’s-degree-granting institutions in the United States. Wherever possible, we rely on the same public sources that the U.S. Department of Education, accreditors, and researchers use.

Found an error? Email the data team and we’ll look into it and, when warranted, issue a correction.

Scope & Institutions

The tracker covers Title-IV eligible, four-year institutions in the 50 states and DC whose highest offering is a bachelor’s degree or higher. We exclude institutions that reported fewer than three years of data over the past decade, institutions flagged as closed, and specialty schools (e.g., seminaries, standalone medical schools).

Financial Indicators

Revenue, expenses, net tuition per FTE, endowment, and federal grants and contracts come from IPEDS Finance surveys (F1A for private not-for-profit, F2 for public, and F3 for for-profit). Dollar figures are adjusted to 2025 dollars using the CPI-U from the Bureau of Labor Statistics. The Federal Composite Financial Score comes directly from annual filings published by the U.S. Department of Education’s Federal Student Aid office.

Enrollment & Staffing

Enrollment uses the IPEDS 12-month unduplicated headcount. International enrollment is split into undergraduate and graduate using IPEDS Fall Enrollment. Staffing uses the IPEDS Human Resources component, reporting full-time and part-time headcounts, with “instructional staff” defined as those with primary responsibility for instruction (including those who also do research).

College Cuts

Layoffs, program suspensions, teach-outs, and closures are drawn from the College Cuts Tracker, supplemented by reporting from The Hechinger Report and verified against institutional communications, board minutes, WARN Act filings, and news reports. Each entry lists at least one primary source. Have you heard of a cut at your university? Submit information here.

Accreditation Actions

Accreditation actions are compiled from the six regional accreditors recognized by the U.S. Department of Education (HLC, MSCHE, NECHE, NWCCU, SACSCOC, WASC SCUC). We include warnings, probations, notices of concern, show-cause actions, teach-out approvals, reaffirmations, and the removal or lifting of any of the above. Specialized programmatic accreditors are not currently covered.

Research Funding Cuts

Grant terminations, freezes, and at-risk awards come from the Grant Witness database, covering grants from the National Institutes of Health, National Science Foundation, Environmental Protection Agency, SAMHSA, and the CDC. We use Grant Witness’s “still disrupted” fields and exclude grants where the university is passing funds on to community groups; we also filter out grants that distributed money after the termination date identified by Grant Witness. Legal challenges remain active in many cases.

Limitations

IPEDS data typically lag by one to two academic years. Accreditation actions and news-based cuts are updated continuously but may miss private or unreported events. Financial indicators are not a verdict on an institution’s viability — they are early warning signals meant to be read in context alongside local reporting and institutional communications.

Downloads & Reuse

Every institution page and landing table has a download button that produces a CSV of the full filtered list — every matching row across all pages, not just the visible page. For bulk access or questions about reuse and attribution, email the data team. Please credit The Hechinger Report and link back to this tracker.
